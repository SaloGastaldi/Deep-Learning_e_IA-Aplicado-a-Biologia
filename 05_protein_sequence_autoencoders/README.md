# 🧬 Protein Sequence Autoencoders & Latent Space Modeling

This project explores representation learning for protein families using convolutional autoencoders trained on Multiple Sequence Alignments (MSAs) of truncated hemoglobins.

The workflow focuses on learning compact latent representations of protein sequences, analyzing the biological structure captured in latent space, and generating novel protein-like sequences through latent space sampling and interpolation.

The project combines deep learning, unsupervised representation learning, sequence generation, and biological interpretation in a fully reproducible PyTorch pipeline.

---

# 🎯 Objectives

* Train convolutional autoencoders on protein MSAs
* Learn compact latent representations of protein families
* Evaluate reconstruction fidelity under different latent dimensions
* Analyze biological organization of the learned latent space
* Generate new protein sequences from latent representations
* Explore amino acid embeddings learned by the model
* Compare trained vs untrained latent representations

---

# 🧬 Biological Context

The project uses a Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA) of truncated hemoglobins (groups N, O, P and Q), which are small heme-binding proteins found across bacteria and microorganisms.

These proteins share conserved structural and functional motifs while exhibiting evolutionary diversity across subfamilies.

The computational objective is:

> Learn biologically meaningful representations directly from aligned protein sequences using unsupervised deep learning.

This type of representation learning is closely related to modern protein language models and generative AI approaches used in computational biology and protein engineering.

# ⚙️ Technical Approach

## Deep Learning Architecture

* 1D Convolutional Autoencoder
* PyTorch implementation
* One-hot encoded protein sequences
* 21-channel representation (20 amino acids + gap)
* Symmetric encoder-decoder architecture
* Latent bottleneck representation

## Training Strategy

* CrossEntropyLoss reconstruction objective
* Adam optimizer
* Mini-batch training using DataLoader
* GPU acceleration support (CUDA)

## Latent Space Analysis

* Sequence reconstruction evaluation
* Latent dimensionality experiments
* PCA projection of embeddings
* Sequence logo generation from latent averages
* Amino acid embedding analysis
* Protein sequence generation via:

  * random latent sampling
  * latent interpolation

# 🧪 Experiments Summary

## 1. Autoencoder Reconstruction Performance

Autoencoders were trained using multiple latent dimensions:

* latent_dim = 8
* latent_dim = 4
* latent_dim = 2
* latent_dim = 1

Sequence reconstruction quality was evaluated using sequence identity between original and reconstructed sequences.

### Key Findings

* Latent dimensions 8, 4, and 2 achieved near-perfect reconstruction
* Even highly compressed latent spaces preserved most biological information
* Severe compression (latent_dim = 1) reduced reconstruction quality significantly

👉 The protein family exhibits strong structural redundancy and can be represented in a compact latent space.

---

## 2. Trained vs Untrained Autoencoder

A comparison between trained and randomly initialized autoencoders showed:

* Trained models reconstruct protein sequences almost perfectly
* Untrained models produce nearly random outputs
* Training organizes latent space into biologically meaningful representations

### Key Insight

The learned latent representations emerge entirely from unsupervised exposure to sequence conservation patterns within the MSA.

---

## 3. External Sequence Evaluation

The trained autoencoder was evaluated on external aligned globin-related sequences, including:

* Human hemoglobin alpha (HBA)
* Human hemoglobin beta (HBB)
* Cytochromes

The model successfully reconstructed all evaluated external sequences with extremely high identity.

### Biological Interpretation

The latent space learned by the autoencoder generalizes beyond the original training subset and captures conserved protein-family level features.

---

## 4. Latent Space Interpretation via Sequence Logos

Sequence logos were generated by:

1. Encoding protein sequences into latent space
2. Averaging latent representations
3. Decoding the mean latent vector
4. Visualizing amino acid probabilities

Comparisons between trained and untrained models showed:

* Structured conservation patterns in trained models
* Biologically coherent residue distributions
* Near-random amino acid probabilities in untrained models

### Group-Specific Logos

Separate logos were generated for protein groups:

* N
* O
* P
* Q

The model captured subgroup-specific conservation signatures while preserving shared globin-family features.

---

## 5. 2D Latent Space Visualization

A latent dimension of 2 was used to directly visualize sequence organization in latent space.

Each sequence was represented as a 2D embedding and colored according to its biological subgroup.

### Key Findings

* Groups N, O and P partially overlap
* Group Q appears separated from the others
* The latent space captures evolutionary and structural relationships

👉 The autoencoder organizes protein sequences according to biologically meaningful similarity patterns despite fully unsupervised training.

---

## 6. PCA Analysis of Latent Embeddings

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was applied to sequence embeddings extracted from the latent space.

### Results

* The first principal component explained nearly all latent variance
* The latent manifold displayed an approximately one-dimensional organization
* Protein subgroups remained partially separable in PCA space

### Interpretation

The autoencoder learned a highly structured and compact representation of sequence variability.

---

## 7. Protein Sequence Generation

Two sequence generation strategies were explored.

### A. Random Latent Sampling

New latent vectors were generated using Gaussian sampling from the learned latent distribution.

Results:

* Generated sequences resembled protein-like patterns
* Some sampled sequences lacked biological plausibility
* BLAST searches showed limited similarity for some random samples

### B. Latent Space Interpolation

Latent vectors from two real sequences were interpolated and decoded.

Results:

* Generated sequences preserved globin-like characteristics
* BLAST searches returned significant matches to truncated globins
* Interpolated sequences remained biologically coherent

### Key Insight

Latent interpolation produces more realistic protein sequences than unconstrained random sampling.

---

## 8. Amino Acid Embedding Analysis

Embeddings for amino acids were extracted from the trained latent representations and projected into 2D using PCA.

### Trained Model

The learned embedding space showed biologically meaningful organization:

* Hydrophobic residues clustered together
* Aromatic residues occupied related regions
* Charged amino acids separated from hydrophobic groups
* Glycine and Proline appeared isolated due to structural specificity

### Untrained Model

Embeddings from the untrained model appeared random and unstructured.

### Biological Interpretation

The autoencoder learned physicochemical and evolutionary relationships between amino acids directly from sequence data.
